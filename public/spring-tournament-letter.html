A Letter From the Owner

BTB Families,

This is the weekend we've been building toward since the fall. Early mornings, hard practices, and countless reps in the driveway are about to show up under the lights. I am genuinely excited to watch these kids compete this spring — and I'm proud of the work they've put in to get here.

Before we start the season, I want to be clear about the standards we carry into every tournament. These aren't suggestions. They are how we operate. When you wear this shield, you represent every family, coach, and player in this club — and I expect us to rise to that every single weekend.

Tournament Standards

Arrival Time

Players arrive one hour before the first game. Not 45 minutes. Not "on the way." One hour. Warm-ups, stretching, and the coach's pre-game are non-negotiable parts of competing at this level.

Before you leave the house, check the bag: helmet, shoulder pads, arm pads, gloves, stick, mouthguard, cleats, both jerseys, and water. If something gets left at home, we do not hold up the team. Showing up prepared is the first thing we ask of every BTB player.

Tailgating

Our tailgates are one of the best parts of BTB weekends — but they only work when every family contributes. Communicate early with your team parents so we are not doubled up on chips and short on real food.

Please keep the junk food to a minimum, and monitor what your child eats between games. Candy, soda, and a pile of cookies does not lead to a strong third game. These kids are competing. Feed them like it.

Representation of the Club

When you wear the shield, you represent all of us. That means respect — toward referees, opponents, tournament staff, and the families around you — at all times. Good calls, bad calls, wins, and losses.

If an issue arises, do what is best for the club. Not what feels right in the moment. That standard is the reason BTB is respected across Long Island and beyond, and I will not let it slip.

Playing Time

Playing time decisions are strictly up to the coaching staff. Our coaches prepare, evaluate, and make the call that gives the team the best chance to compete. That is their job.

If you want to discuss your child's playing time, you must wait until the next day. No sideline conversations. No parking lot conversations. No post-game texts. This rule protects our coaches, our kids, and the quality of the conversation. It is not optional.

Support the Kids. Support the Coaches. Support the Club.

Cheer loud. Be positive. Lift each other up. Your child is watching how you act more closely than you realize — so give them something to model.

Then enjoy the weekend. Enjoy the tailgates, the memories, and watching your kid grow into a better player, teammate, and person. That is why we do this.

Thank you for trusting Be The Best with your family. We don't take that lightly. Hold the standard, support each other, and let's make this spring one to remember.

See you on the field.

— Dan Achatz
Founder & Owner • Be The Best Lacrosse Club
